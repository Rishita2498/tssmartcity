About Us

The word "Vivers" means food and "Cartle" means organization.The main motto of "Vivers Cartle" committe is to help the starving by serving the food which is being wasted everyday. Vivers cartle serves as basis between the food donor and NGOs. According to FAO estimates in "The State of Food Security and Nutrition in the World,2017" report, 14.5% of the population is undernourished in India.Also 51.4% of women in reproductive age between 15-45 yrs are anaemic,etc. On the other hand, it is estimated that nearly one third of the food produced in the world for human consumption every year gets wasted.Some food is also wasted on the shelves and in the warehouses of the food businesses either due to excess production,introduction of new products,labeling errors or due to shorter remaining shelf life. Such food could be salvaged by timely withdrawing it from the distribution network,aggregating it and the redirecting it to the people in need. So we have come up with an idea to reduce the starvation in our city(Hyderabad). Since some of the people are not aware of the process of donating food and some wont find time to reach out the organizations,etc they can contact our commitee members through the website. The committe members in the near by locality of the donor, collects the food and hand it over to the NGOs with in short span of time who helps the needy.Various NGOs such as Save the Children,Feeding India,Akshaya patra etc will be linked with our committee.Though the donor could directly contact the NGOs, it is bit a time taking process because they are assigned to works and they might not be in the nearby location of the donor.Hence to save the time,prevent the spoilage of food and help the starving... we are here. To encourage the donors we do gift them based on the points given to them everytime they donate. Also we do give titles such as "Patron of the month","Patron of the year" to the donors to make them recognized.People who are willing to provide service can join us with their corresponding details.
